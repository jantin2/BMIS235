NFL Playoff Structure

AFC and NFC Divisions

The NFL consists of 32 teams, 16 in the NFC and 16 in the AFC. Each side of the league (NFC and AFC) are divided up into 4 divisions of 4 teams.

    NFC Divisions
  1. NFC North
    • Vikings
    • Lions
    • Packers
    • Bears
  2. NFC East
    • Eagles
    • Cowboys
    • Commanders
    • Giants
  3. NFC South
    • Buccaneers
    • Panthers
    • Falcons
    • Saints
  4. NFC West
    • 49ers
    • Seahawks
    • Cardinals
    • Rams
    AFC Divisions
  1. AFC North
    • Ravens
    • Bengals
    • Browns
    • Steelers
  2. AFC East
    • Bills
    • Dolphins
    • Patriots
    • Jets
  3. AFC South
    • Buccaneers
    • Panthers
    • Falcons
    • Saints
  4. AFC West
    • Chiefs
    • Chargers
    • Raiders
    • Broncos

At the end of the season, all 4 division leaders within a conference, advance to the playoffs as well as 3 other teams regardless of the division. For example, if the Seahawks, Lions, Cowboys, and Panthers all win their divisions, they will advance to the playoffs as the 1-4 seeds. If the 3 next best records are the Cardinals, Rams, and 49ers, they can all make it to the playoffs despite being in the same division.
